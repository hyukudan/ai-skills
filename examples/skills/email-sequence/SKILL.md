# Email Sequence Design

You create email sequences that nurture relationships and drive conversion.

## Core Principles

**One email, one job.** Single primary purpose, one main CTA.

**Value before ask.** Lead with usefulness, earn the right to sell.

**Relevance over volume.** Fewer, better emails win.

**Clear path forward.** Every email moves them somewhere.

---

## Sequence Parameters

**Length:**
- Welcome: 3-7 emails
- Lead nurture: 5-10 emails
- Onboarding: 5-10 emails
- Re-engagement: 3-5 emails

**Timing:**
- Welcome: Immediate
- Early sequence: 1-2 days apart
- Nurture: 2-4 days apart
- Long-term: Weekly

**Subject lines:** Clear > Clever, 40-60 characters

---

## Welcome Sequence Template

**Email 1: Welcome (Immediate)**
- Deliver what was promised
- Single next action
- Set expectations

**Email 2: Quick Win (Day 1-2)**
- Enable small success
- Build confidence

**Email 3: Story/Why (Day 3-4)**
- Origin story or mission
- Show you understand their problem

**Email 4: Social Proof (Day 5-6)**
- Case study or testimonial
- Soft CTA to explore

**Email 5: Objection (Day 7-8)**
- Address common hesitation
- Show easy path forward

**Email 6: Core Feature (Day 9-11)**
- Highlight underused capability
- Direct CTA to try it

**Email 7: Conversion (Day 12-14)**
- Summarize value
- Clear offer, urgency if appropriate

---

## Lead Nurture Template

1. **Deliver + Introduce** (Immediate)
2. **Expand on Topic** (Day 2-3)
3. **Problem Deep-Dive** (Day 4-5)
4. **Solution Framework** (Day 6-8)
5. **Case Study** (Day 9-11)
6. **Differentiation** (Day 12-14)
7. **Objection Handler** (Day 15-18)
8. **Direct Offer** (Day 19-21)

---

## Re-engagement Template

1. **Check-In** (Day 30-60 inactive): "Is everything okay?"
2. **Value Reminder** (Day 2-3): What's new, remind of past value
3. **Incentive** (Day 5-7): Offer if appropriate
4. **Last Chance** (Day 10-14): "Should we stop emailing?" One-click to stay/go

---

## Email Types Reference

**Onboarding:** New user activation, new customer welcome, setup reminders

**Retention:** Upgrade prompts, ask for review, usage reports, NPS surveys

**Billing:** Switch to annual, failed payment recovery, renewal reminders, cancellation survey

**Win-back:** Expired trials, cancelled customers

**Campaigns:** Newsletter, promotions, product updates, pricing changes

---

## Email Copy Structure

1. **Hook:** First line grabs attention
2. **Context:** Why this matters to them
3. **Value:** The useful content
4. **CTA:** What to do next
5. **Sign-off:** Human, warm close

**Format:**
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- White space between sections
- Bullets for scannability
- Mobile-first

**Length:**
- Transactional: 50-125 words
- Educational: 150-300 words
- Story-driven: 300-500 words

---

## Subject Line Patterns

- **Question:** "Still struggling with X?"
- **How-to:** "How to [outcome] in [timeframe]"
- **Number:** "3 ways to [benefit]"
- **Direct:** "[Name], your [thing] is ready"
- **Story:** "The mistake I made with [topic]"

---

## Personalization

**Merge fields:** First name (fallback: "there"), company, usage data

**Triggers:** Action-based sends > time-based (feature used, milestone hit, inactivity)

**Segments:** By behavior (active/inactive), stage (trial/paid), profile (industry/role)

---

## Metrics

- Open rate: 20-40% benchmark
- Click rate: 2-5% benchmark
- Unsubscribe: Keep under 0.5%

**Test:** Subject lines (highest impact), send times, length, CTA placement
