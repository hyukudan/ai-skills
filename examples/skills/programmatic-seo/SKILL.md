# Programmatic SEO

You build SEO-optimized pages at scale that rank, provide value, and avoid thin content penalties.

## Core Principles

**1. Unique value per page** — Not just swapped variables. Each page must provide real depth.

**2. Proprietary data wins** — Data competitors can't replicate: product-derived, user-generated, exclusive.

**3. Subfolders, not subdomains** — `yoursite.com/templates/` passes authority. Subdomains are separate sites.

**4. Match search intent** — Pages must actually answer what people search for.

**5. Quality over quantity** — 100 great pages beat 10,000 thin ones.

## The 12 Playbooks

### 1. Templates
**Pattern:** "[type] template"
**Example:** `/templates/resume/`, `/templates/invoice/`
**Why it works:** High intent, downloadable assets

### 2. Curation
**Pattern:** "best [category]"
**Example:** `/best/website-builders/`
**Why it works:** Comparison shoppers, commercial intent

### 3. Conversions
**Pattern:** "[X] to [Y]"
**Example:** `/convert/usd-to-gbp/`, `/pdf-to-word/`
**Why it works:** Instant utility, high volume

### 4. Comparisons
**Pattern:** "[X] vs [Y]", "[X] alternative"
**Example:** `/compare/notion-vs-coda/`
**Why it works:** Purchase intent

### 5. Examples
**Pattern:** "[type] examples"
**Example:** `/examples/landing-pages/`
**Why it works:** Research phase, shareable

### 6. Locations
**Pattern:** "[service] in [location]"
**Example:** `/coworking/san-diego/`
**Why it works:** Local intent scales with geography

### 7. Personas
**Pattern:** "[product] for [audience]"
**Example:** `/solutions/startups/`, `/for/agencies/`
**Why it works:** Speaks directly to searcher context

### 8. Integrations
**Pattern:** "[your product] [other product] integration"
**Example:** `/integrations/slack/`
**Why it works:** Captures users of other products

### 9. Glossary
**Pattern:** "what is [term]"
**Example:** `/glossary/api/`
**Why it works:** Top-of-funnel, establishes expertise

### 10. Translations
**Pattern:** Same content in multiple languages
**Example:** `/es/`, `/de/`
**Why it works:** New markets, lower competition

### 11. Directory
**Pattern:** "[category] tools"
**Example:** `/directory/email-marketing/`
**Why it works:** Research capture, link magnet

### 12. Profiles
**Pattern:** "[entity name]"
**Example:** `/companies/stripe/`
**Why it works:** Informational traffic, topical authority

## Choosing Your Playbook

| If you have... | Consider... |
|----------------|-------------|
| Proprietary data | Stats, Directories, Profiles |
| Product integrations | Integrations |
| Design product | Templates, Examples |
| Multi-segment audience | Personas |
| Local presence | Locations |
| Utility product | Conversions |
| Content/expertise | Glossary, Curation |
| Competitor landscape | Comparisons |

**Combine playbooks:** "Best coworking spaces in San Diego" = Curation + Locations

## Implementation

### Template Design

- Header with target keyword
- Unique intro (not just variable swap)
- Data-driven sections
- Related pages / internal links
- Intent-appropriate CTAs

### Ensuring Uniqueness

- Conditional content based on data
- User-generated content
- Original insights per page
- Remove pages with no unique value

### Internal Linking

- Hub: Main category page
- Spokes: Individual programmatic pages
- Cross-links between related spokes
- No orphan pages

---

## Quality Checklist

**Content:**
- [ ] Unique value per page
- [ ] Answers search intent
- [ ] Readable and useful

**Technical:**
- [ ] Unique titles/meta descriptions
- [ ] Proper heading structure
- [ ] Schema markup
- [ ] Correct canonicals

**Linking:**
- [ ] Connected to site architecture
- [ ] In XML sitemap
- [ ] No orphans

---

## Avoid

**Thin content:** Just swapping city names in identical content

**Cannibalization:** Multiple pages targeting same keyword

**Over-generation:** Creating pages with no search demand

**Poor data:** Outdated, incorrect, or blank information
